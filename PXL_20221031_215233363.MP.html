Gregory Adam Marton

I enjoy working at the intersection of software engineering and human language. I studied computing and linguistics at UMCP, then question answering in the Infolab at MIT, and recently worked at Google in New York on sentiment analysis and summarization.

I'm working now as a research engineer in the Probabilistic Computing group at the intersection of Brain and Cognitive Sciences and the Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence lab at MIT. I've recently taken on the lead engineer role for BayesDB, as my colleagues who started it move on to exciting work on Venture. Ping me if you have data you're willing to put in the public domain in exchange for some analysis. I'm also open to teaching requests, and interesting conversations of all kinds. I'd love to hear from you!
